# Shadowrun (6th Edition) — First Adventure Walkthrough

## System Overview

Shadowrun is a cyberpunk-fantasy hybrid RPG set in 2080, where megacorporations rule, magic has returned, and elves run data heists alongside trolls with assault rifles. You play shadowrunners — deniable assets hired for jobs corps can't officially sanction. The AI acts as your Game Master, narrating the sprawl, voicing fixers and corp security, and adjudicating the layered physical/Matrix/astral action system.

## Setting Up Your Campaign

1. **Create a campaign.** Use the `create_campaign` tool with ruleset `shadowrun`. Name it after your crew or city — "Seattle After Dark", "The Aztlan Run", "Chrome and Bone".
2. **Create your character.** Choose your metatype (Human, Elf, Dwarf, Ork, Troll) and priority spread (ABCDE assigned to Metatype, Attributes, Magic/Resonance, Skills, Resources). Distribute your attribute points and skill points. Note your starting nuyen for gear.
3. **Start a session.** Name it after the job — "Cold Delivery", "Ghost in the Foundation", "Mr. Johnson's Favor".

## Suggested Opening Prompt

> "I'm playing Cipher, a human Decker with Logic 6, Willpower 4. My fixer, a troll named Boomer, has set up a meeting with a Mr. Johnson at a noodle bar in Puyallup. My team includes a street sam named Kestrel and a shaman called Moth. We arrive five minutes early and scope the place."

The AI will set the meet, describe the job offer, and let your team ask questions before accepting.

## Key Mechanics to Establish Early

- **Dice pools:** Build a pool of d6s equal to Attribute + Skill (+ modifiers). Roll them all. Each 5 or 6 is a hit. Count hits vs. a threshold (or opponent's hits). More hits = better result.
- **Glitches:** If more than half your dice show 1s, it's a glitch (complication). If you also got zero hits, it's a critical glitch (disaster).
- **Edge:** A metacurrency that refreshes each scene. Spend 1 to add 2 dice to a pool, reroll failures, or go first in initiative. Spend more for bigger effects. Gain Edge by acting in character, exploiting your enemy's weakness, or completing objectives.
- **Matrix actions (Deckers/Technomancers):** The Matrix runs parallel to physical space. Your Decker can jack in and take actions there while the team acts physically. Ask the AI to run Matrix encounters as a separate layer.
- **Magic (Awakened):** Mages and shamans cast spells using Magic + Spellcasting dice. Physical adepts spend Power Points on passive abilities. Astral space can be perceived and traveled. Ask the AI to narrate astral encounters.
- **Combat:** Initiative = Reaction + Intuition + d6. Each combat turn you get a major action and a minor action. Ranged attacks use Agility + Firearms vs. a defense pool.

## A Classic First Session Arc

**Act 1 — The Meet:** Mr. Johnson offers the job. Negotiate pay (Con + Charisma or Negotiation). Ask about complications — the AI will hint at what's not being said.

**Act 2 — Legwork:** Research the target — Matrix searches (Electronics + Computer), street contacts (Con + Charisma), astral recon (Awakened characters). Each lead reduces risk in Act 3.

**Act 3 — The Run:** Execute the plan. One major obstacle — physical security, IC in the Matrix, a spirit guardian. Exfiltrate with the objective. The Johnson pays, probably without a double-cross. Probably.

## Tips

- Build your dice pool explicitly: "I'm using Agility 5 + Pistols 4, that's 9 dice." The AI needs these numbers.
- Tell the AI your contacts at character creation — they're your network, not just flavor.
- Noise reduces Matrix dice pools at range. Ask the AI the noise rating before jacking in remotely.
- Karma (experience) comes after jobs. Spend it to raise skills and attributes between runs.
- The first run should be achievable. Save the double-crosses for session 3.
