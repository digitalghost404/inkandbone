# Blades in the Dark — First Adventure Walkthrough

## System Overview

Blades in the Dark is a heist RPG set in Duskwall — a gas-lit city sealed behind electroplasmic barriers, haunted by ghosts and run by criminal factions. You play a crew of scoundrels pulling jobs, building your gang's reputation, and struggling against rival factions and the city's supernatural darkness. The AI acts as your Game Master, running the city and its factions, voicing inspectors and crime lords, and adjudicating the flashback-driven action system that defines Blades.

## Setting Up Your Campaign

1. **Create a campaign.** Use the `create_campaign` tool with ruleset `blades`. Name it after your crew — "The Lampblack Accord", "Silver Knives of Crow's Foot", "The Forgotten Coin".
2. **Create your character.** Use the `create_character` tool with just your character's name — ink & bone automatically distributes 4 points among the 12 Action Ratings (each 0–2) and sets Stress, Coin, Load, and XP tracks. Tell the AI your Playbook and Vice so it can shape your abilities and the crew's style. Adjust any values with `update_character`.
3. **Start a session.** Name it after the job — "The Ironhook Score", "Blood in Barrowcleft", "What the Dimmer Twins Owe".

## Suggested Opening Prompt

> "I'm playing Nyryx, a Slide (con artist and face) with Consort 2, Sway 2, Deceive 2, Finesse 1. My crew is small — just me and a Lurk named Shade. We've been offered our first real job: steal a debt ledger from a minor merchant lord named Tarvul who owes money to a more dangerous man. We're meeting our employer in a teahouse in Brightstone."

The AI will run the employer meeting, define the score's parameters, and help you plan the approach.

## Key Mechanics to Establish Early

- **Action rolls:** When you do something risky, roll a number of d6s equal to your Action Rating (0 = roll 2 dice, take lowest). Result: 1–3 = bad outcome, 4–5 = partial success (with cost), 6 = success, double 6 = critical.
- **Position and Effect:** Before any roll, the AI establishes Position (Desperate, Risky, Controlled) and Effect (Limited, Standard, Great). Position determines consequences; Effect determines how much you accomplish. Adjust these by describing how you set up the action.
- **Stress:** Spending Stress lets you boost rolls (add dice), resist consequences, or activate special abilities. Push Yourself (+2 dice, 2 Stress) or take a Devil's Bargain (bonus die, with a narrative cost from the AI).
- **Flashbacks:** You can call a Flashback at any time during the job to establish something your character prepared before the score. "I bribed the night watchman two days ago." The AI assigns a Stress cost based on how plausible it is.
- **Clocks:** The AI tracks progress and danger as pie-chart clocks — fill all segments and the thing happens. Alarm clocks, job progress clocks, faction threat clocks. Ask the AI what clocks are ticking.

## A Classic First Session Arc

**Act 1 — The Score Setup:** Meet the employer. Learn the target, the constraints, and what's being left unsaid. Gather information — Gather Info rolls to learn the floor plan, the guards' schedule, or the target's habits.

**Act 2 — The Job:** Approach the target location and begin the heist. Each obstacle is a roll with Position and Effect. Use Flashbacks to handle complications you didn't plan for in detail.

**Act 3 — The Payoff:** Extract with the score. Downtime follows — recover Stress, reduce Heat, pursue your Vice, advance your Crew clock and XP.

## Tips

- Do not over-plan. Blades rewards improvisation and Flashbacks. Plan your approach, not every step.
- Heat tracks how much attention law enforcement pays to your crew. High Heat = harder jobs. Lay low between scores.
- Trauma accumulates when you can't reduce Stress. Four Trauma marks and your character retires (or worse). Protect your Stress.
- Faction standing matters. Ask the AI about the factions active in your neighborhood — the Lampblacks, Red Sashes, Bluecoats — and manage your relationships with them.
- The city is alive with ghosts and the electroplasmic Leviathan hunters. Lean into the setting's weirdness.
